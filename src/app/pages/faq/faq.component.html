Turnaround & Production

What qualifies as a rush job, how fast we can move, and what we can handle at volume.

What counts as an emergency print job?

Any job where the deadline is the priority. Some clients need 500 labels by tomorrow. Others need 10,000 packaging sleeves within the week. If your timeline is compressed and a standard printer cannot meet it, contact Fresh Prints. We evaluate every job individually and give you an honest answer about what we can deliver.

How fast can Fresh Prints turn around a job?

Many jobs are completed same-day or next-day. Larger or more complex jobs, such as high-volume packaging runs or multi-component collateral kits, may require several days. Turnaround depends on the job type, quantity, file readiness and your specific deadline. Contact us immediately so we can assess your situation.

Can you handle high-volume packaging and label orders on a rush timeline?

Yes. High-volume packaging sleeves, custom labels and product stickers are among our most common rush jobs. Quantity alone does not disqualify a job from emergency production. Tell us what you need and we will tell you what is possible.

Files & Artwork

What to send us and what to do if your files are not ready.

What file formats do you accept for rush jobs?

Print-ready PDF files are preferred. AI and EPS files are also accepted. If your files are not print-ready, contact us as soon as possible so we can determine what is feasible within your deadline.
